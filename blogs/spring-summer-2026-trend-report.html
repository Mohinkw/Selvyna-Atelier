SS26 Trend #1: Crochet everything

Crochet moves beyond festival wear into everyday dressing—tanks, cardigans, beach cover-ups, and even dresses.

Why it's trending

  • Nostalgic Y2K revival meets cottagecore aesthetic.
  • TikTok #crochettop hit 450M views in 2025; momentum continues.
  • Sustainable positioning: handmade feel, artisanal appeal.

What to produce

  • Crochet tank tops: Cropped or standard length; halter or spaghetti strap.
  • Open-knit cardigans: Oversized, longline; layering piece for spring.
  • Crochet beach dresses: Maxi or midi; worn over swimwear.
  • Crochet bikini tops: Triangle or bandeau style.

Manufacturing specs

  • Fabric: 100% cotton yarn (3–5mm gauge) or cotton-acrylic blend for stretch.
  • Technique: Machine crochet for consistency and scale; hand-crochet for premium lines (3x cost).
  • MOQ: 200–300 unit's per style (crochet is labor-intensive).
  • Lead time: 8–10 weeks (longer than standard knit's due to crochet process).
  • Cost: $6–12 per unit depending on complexity.

Launch timing

  • Order by: January 2026
  • Launch: March–April 2026 (early spring)

How to choose which trends to produce

don't chase every trend. Use this decision framework:

1) Align with your brand identity

  • If you're a minimalist brand, focus on coastal grandmother, linen blazers, and neutral pastels.
  • If you're streetwear/Gen Z-focused, prioritize cargo skirts, micro shorts, and cut-out tops.

2) Test demand before scaling

  • Order 100–200 unit's of 1–2 trend styles.
  • Run ads to your email list or cold traffic for 2 weeks.
  • If sellthrough hit's 60–70%, reorder 300–500 unit's.

3) Consider manufacturing complexity

  • Crochet, blazers, and sheer layers require higher MOQs and longer lead times.
  • Cargo shorts, micro shorts, and matching sets are easier to produce quickly.

4) Avoid oversaturation

  • If Amazon already has 500+ listings for "butter yellow dress," differentiate with unique details (ruffles, tiered, cut-outs).
  • Use niche positioning (e.g., "petite butter yellow dress" or "plus-size cargo skirt").
